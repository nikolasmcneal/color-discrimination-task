Hello! I am a third-year undergraduate student in the College of Arts and Sciences at The Ohio State University. My research interests include computational neuroscience, machine learning, and decision science. I enjoy broadening my data science skills in a manner that aligns with my teamwork-based, cutting-edge, and interactive approach. I love reading and learning about human brains, artificial intelligence, and the world in between. I intend on pursuing a graduate education following the completion of my undergraduate degree.

In addition, I am an Eminence Fellow and a Stamps Scholar. Feel free to read about my Eminence group's environmental service organization here.

Research Overview

Since May 2020, I have been participating in Dr. Tai Sing Lee's computer science lab at Carnegie Mellon University. This summer, I will be working on developing a biologically plausible recurrent neural network that is representative of the hierarchial neuronal structure and function in cortical neurophysiology.

In addition, since December 2019, I have been an undergraduate researcher in Dr. Ian Krajbich's neuroeconomics laboratory. I am working on a color discrimination task that seeks to analyze the difference in response time behavior when incentivized differently. I also built a lexical decision speed-accuracy-tradeoff web-based experiment. In this lab, we use a drift diffusion model, fitting our response time and accuracy data and retrieving the rate of evidence accumulation, the "boundary separation" threshold, and non-decision time.

In the past, I have been involved in other technical research projects. For much of my second year, I collaborated on some machine learning models for the Autonomous Computing and Memory Materials group (ACAMM), alongside collaborators at MIT, Johns Hopkins, and UCSD. This project is a part of the multi-university initiative to introduce biologically-inspired replacements for inefficient silicon-based hardware.

In my spare time, I enjoy contributing to various short-term research projects. I am helping the Ohio State Music Cognition Lab by developing a framework inspired by jsPsych to sort audio stimuli. In Summer 2020, I worked for Dr. Skyler Cranmer on a computational linguistics project to determine gun violence partisan bias from media report transcripts.
